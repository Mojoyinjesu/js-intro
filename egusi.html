Egusi Soup

Ingredients

  • Ground melon seeds
  • Palm Oil
  • Meat(beef, chicken, goat)
  • Stcokfish
  • Crayfish
  • Vegetables(ugu, Spinach, Bitterleaf)
  • Seasoning cubes, salt
  • Onions, pepper(scotch bonnet, habenaro), tomatoes, iru(locust beans)

Instructions

  1. Pre-boil meats with some onion, salt and bouillon until tender. Reserve the stock. Debone any smoked fish and remove skin if desired
  2. Mix ground egusi with a little water to make a thick paste or keep it dry to sprinkle during frying (both methods work). Some cooks mix egusi with ground pepper and onions.
  3. In a heavy pot, heat palm oil over medium heat until hot but not smoking. Add chopped onions and sauté briefly. Add egusi paste (or sprinkle in dry egusi) and fry, stirring constantly, until it turns slightly brown and releases nutty aroma, 6-10 minutes. If using dry egusi, fry until it firms up.
  4. Pour in reserved meat stock gradually (start small) and stir to loosen the egusi. This creates the soup base. You want a thick, slightly grainy consistency; adjust liquid to preference.
  5. Add pre-cooked meats, smoked fish, ground crayfish, bouillon, salt & pepper. Let simmer 8-12 minutes so flavors meld. If too thick, add more stock/water.
  6. Stir in chopped leafy greens and simmer 3-5 minutes until wilted but still bright.
  7. Taste and correct seasoning. Reduce heat and simmer for another 2–3 minutes. Remove from heat and serve.

Serve with:

  • Pounded Yam
  • Eba
  • Amala
