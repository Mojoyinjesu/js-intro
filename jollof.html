Jollof Rice

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • vegetable oil
  • Seasoning(curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt, seasonsing cube, garlic)
  • Stock
  • Onions, Bell peppers, tomatoes, tomato paste

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice under cold water until water runs mostly clear. Set aside to drain.
  2. Roughly chop tomatoes and peppers and blend to a smooth puree. If using canned, skip blending.
  3. Heat oil in a large, heavy pot over medium heat. Sauté half the chopped onion until translucent (3-4 min). Add garlic and tomato paste — fry the paste 2-3 minutes to remove raw taste
  4. Pour in the blended tomatoes/pepper. Bring to a simmer and let cook until the mixture reduces and oil separates on top (20-25 minutes). Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
  5. Add thyme, bay leaves, curry powder, smoked paprika, bouillon, salt & pepper. Taste and adjust
  6. Stir in the drained rice to coat grains with the sauce. Add chicken stock, liquid level should be about 1-2 cm above rice (you’ll generally add ~3-4 cups depending on rice). Stir once.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover pot with lid (or foil + lid) and simmer for 18-25 minutes until rice has absorbed liquid. Avoid lifting lid too often.
  8. Once cooked, fluff gently with fork, add cooked protein/veggies if using (peas & carrots, shredded chicken) and fold through. Drizzle a little oil if desired and garnish with sliced onions.

Serve with:

  • Fried Plantain
  • Coleslaw
